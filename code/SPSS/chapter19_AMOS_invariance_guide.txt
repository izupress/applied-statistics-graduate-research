CHAPTER 19 AMOS MEASUREMENT-INVARIANCE GUIDE

Data file
  Open data/chapter19_invariance_data.sav.
  Use programme_n as the grouping variable: 1 = Masters, 2 = Doctoral.

Common model
  Define one latent factor measured by q1 through q9.
  Include an intercept and residual term for every indicator.
  Estimate means and intercepts. Use Masters as the reference group.

Model sequence
  1. Configural: same factor pattern; all loadings, intercepts, and
     residual variances free across groups.
  2. Metric: assign identical labels to corresponding loadings.
  3. Scalar: retain loading labels and assign identical labels to all
     corresponding indicator intercepts.
  4. Partial scalar: remove equality labels from q4 and q9 intercepts.
  5. Partial strict: retain partial-scalar constraints and assign equal
     labels to corresponding residual variances.
  6. Equal latent variance: additionally constrain the Doctoral factor
     variance to the Masters factor variance.
  7. Equal latent mean: additionally constrain the Doctoral factor mean
     to zero.

Identification
  In the configural model, fix each group factor variance to one and
  estimate every loading. From the metric model onward, fix the Masters
  factor variance to one, estimate the Doctoral factor variance, and use
  equal loading labels. Fix the Masters factor mean to zero. Estimate the
  Doctoral factor mean only after scalar or partial-scalar invariance.

Requested output
  Chi-square, df, CFI, TLI, RMSEA with 90% CI, SRMR, AIC, modification
  indices, standardised estimates, residual moments, and nested-model
  comparisons. Save every model under a distinct filename.

Decision record
  Compare exact chi-square differences together with changes in CFI,
  RMSEA, and SRMR. The supplied data intentionally contain q4 and q9
  intercept non-invariance. Free those parameters only in the documented
  partial-scalar model and retain the other seven intercepts as anchors.
